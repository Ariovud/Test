Executive Summary

Today we manually disabled 16,177 stale guest accounts that had accumulated in our tenant over several years without detection or review. This operation required dedicated engineering time to audit, script, validate, and execute safely. Without a structural change to how external identities are lifecycle-managed, this situation will recur at the same scale. This document outlines the business case for Microsoft Entra ID Governance as the sustainable solution.

The Problem We Are Solving

Our tenant currently has no automated mechanism to:

	•	Review whether guest users still require access after invitation
	•	Notify internal sponsors when their guests become inactive
	•	Automatically disable or remove external identities that are no longer needed
	•	Conduct access reviews for internal users across privileged roles, groups, and applications
	•	Demonstrate to auditors that access is periodically reviewed and certified

The consequence of this gap is evidenced directly by today’s operation — 16,177 external identities remained active and enabled in our tenant, some for up to five years, with no review, no sponsor accountability, and no visibility. Each of those accounts represented an external authentication point into our environment with no MFA enforced until this week.

This is not a one-time problem. Without automation it will rebuild at the same rate.

What Microsoft Entra ID Governance Provides

Entra ID Governance is Microsoft’s identity lifecycle management platform. The capabilities directly relevant to our environment are:

Access Reviews
Automated periodic reviews of guest accounts, group memberships, privileged role assignments, and application access. Reviewers — either sponsors, managers, or the users themselves — receive automated notifications to confirm ongoing need. Accounts where no response is received can be automatically disabled or removed based on defined policy. This directly replaces the manual operation we performed today.

Entitlement Management
Structured access packages that define what resources a user — internal or external — should have access to, for how long, and under what approval workflow. Guest users can be invited through a governed process that automatically assigns access, sets an expiry date, and triggers renewal or removal at that date. This prevents uncontrolled guest accumulation entirely.

Lifecycle Workflows
Automated identity lifecycle actions triggered by events — joiner, mover, leaver. For guests this means automatic offboarding when a defined period of inactivity is reached, without manual intervention.

Privileged Identity Management — Extended
We already use PIM for Entra role activation. ID Governance extends this to include access reviews of privileged role holders, ensuring that role assignments are periodically certified and unnecessary privilege is removed automatically.

Licensing Options and Cost

There are two paths to acquiring this capability within our existing Microsoft agreement:

Option 1 — Microsoft Entra ID Governance Standalone

	•	Licensed per user per month
	•	Covers both members and guests
	•	Current Microsoft list price: approximately $7 per user per month
	•	Applied to our environment: this would cover our member user population for internal access reviews and our guest population for external lifecycle management

Guests are typically licensed at a ratio — Microsoft’s current model allows up to 5 guests per licensed member user under certain conditions, which given our guest-to-member ratio needs to be validated with our Microsoft account team

Option 2 — Microsoft Entra Suite

	•	Bundles ID Governance with Entra Internet Access, Private Access, and ID Protection
	•	More cost-effective if we intend to mature our Zero Trust network access controls alongside identity governance
	•	Current Microsoft list price: approximately $12 per user per month

Given our existing P1/P2 and E3/E5 investments, our Microsoft account team should be able to provide a delta pricing model based on what we already own

Important note on guest licensing:
Microsoft’s guest licensing model for ID Governance specifically needs to be confirmed with our account team as it has evolved. The current general position is that guests are covered under the MAU (Monthly Active Users) billing model for external identities, but access reviews for guests require the member users who own those reviews to be licensed. I recommend a formal licensing conversation with Microsoft before committing to a number.

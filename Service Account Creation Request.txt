Service Account Creation Request — Field Specification for ServiceNow
This is the functional spec from the IAM side: every field IAM needs captured before a service account can be created, why it's needed, and the logic governing it. Hand this to your ServiceNow team as the requirements — how they build it (catalog item, record producer, etc.) is their decision, not yours to dictate.

Section 1 — Requestor and Routing
FieldTypeMandatoryPurposeRequestor NameAuto-populated (logged-in user)YesIdentifies who raised the requestRequestor Department/DivisionDropdown: Division G / Division M / OtherYesRoutes correctly and confirms which forest/tenant context appliesBusiness OwnerReference field (person lookup)YesThe accountable named individual — cannot be the requestor unless requestor is also the owner; cannot be a generic mailbox or team nameBusiness Owner ManagerAuto-populated from owner's profileYesSecondary contact if owner leaves the organisationAssignment GroupFixed value: IAMYes — locked, not editableEnsures every request lands directly in the IAM queue, no exceptions
Logic: Assignment group field is hard-coded to IAM and not user-selectable. This closes the gap you flagged earlier where help desk or other teams could create service accounts outside IAM control.

Logic — gMSA default enforcement: If "Legacy Service Account" is selected, a mandatory field appears:
FieldTypeMandatoryPurposeJustification for Not Using gMSAFree text, minimum 100 charactersMandatory if Legacy selectedForces explicit technical reason — e.g. "application does not support gMSA authentication," "third-party vendor requirement" — reviewed by IAM before approval
This field existing and being mandatory is itself the control. It makes legacy account creation deliberately harder than gMSA, which is the behaviour you want to drive.
